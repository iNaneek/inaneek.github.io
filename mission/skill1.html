Additive Manufacturing & DFM

A visual look at how I use additive manufacturing and design-for-manufacturability to rapidly develop, validate, and refine engineering solutions.

Optimized Functional Prototypes

I design functional prototypes with intentional geometry, allowing every printed part to perform structurally, interface cleanly with other components, and align with the manufacturing constraints of rapid prototyping. Print orientation, stress paths, material selection, and tolerance design shape every iteration.

Material-Driven Decision Making

I choose materials based on environmental performance, mechanical strength, flexibility, and post-processing needs. From ASA for outdoor durability, to TPU for flexible systems, to PLA for rapid iteration, each project is guided by both engineering requirements and practical manufacturability.

Print-Aware Geometry

My designs incorporate chamfers, bridging strategies, layer-oriented strength paths, tolerance compensation, and assembly simplification. This ensures each print performs reliably and reduces post-processing or support waste.

From Prototype to Production Thinking

Additive manufacturing accelerates early development, but I design with future production in mind. This includes integrating DFM considerations such as injection molding constraints, machining allowances, fastener strategy, modular design, and ergonomic considerations.
